Another random note: Some cool (?) history facts and quotes: 

> "You shall know a word by the company it keeps"

John Rupert Firth allegedly said this in 1957. Firth was a professor of linguistics in London, holding teaching positions in UCL, LSE, and Oxford when Japan went to war with Britain in 1941. Having previously been a staff in the imperial services in India, his job became to devise crash courses in Japanese so that Britain's armed services in Asia, which had institutional knowledge of south and south east Asian languages until then but not in Japanese, could quickly eavesdrop and interpret Japanese radio communications.
